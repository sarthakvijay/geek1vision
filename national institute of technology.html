National Institute of Technology (NITs)

The National Institutes of Technology (NITs) are a group of premier federally-funded public engineering colleges in India. These institutes have been declared by Act of Parliament as institutions of national importance. On their inception decades ago, all NITs were referred to as Regional Engineering Colleges (RECs) and were governed by their respective state governments. NITs were founded to promote regional diversity and multi-cultural understanding in India.
Comprising thirty-one autonomous institutes, they are located in one each major state/territory of India. In 2007, the Indian government declared these schools as Institute of National Importance. NITs offer degree courses at bachelors, masters, and doctorate levels in various branches of engineering and technology. All NITs are autonomous which enables them to set up their own curriculum.

1.National Institute of Technology, CALICUT
Established: 1961(2002)
State: Kerala
NIRF rank on India level: 35
Set in a picturesque landscape of the foothills of the Western Ghats, National Institute of Technology Calicut (NITC) is located about 22 kilometers North East of Calicut City in the state of Kerala, India. NIT Calicut has a host of student clubs that engage the students in various extra-curricular activities. The SAC takes initiative to conduct the Annual Techno- Management Festival of the institute titled 'TATHVA' and the Cultural festival titled 'RAGAM'.

Established: 1960(2003)

NIRF rank on India level: 67

3.Motilal Nehru National Institute of Technology, ALLAHABAD
Established: 1961(2002)
State: Uttar Pradesh
NIRF rank on India level: 23
Motilal Nehru National Institute of Technology Allahabad, Allahabad (MNNIT) is an Institute with total commitment to quality and excellence in academic pursuits. It was established as one of the seventeen Regional Engineering Colleges of India in the year 1961 as a joint enterprise of Government of India and Government of Uttar Pradesh, and was an associated college of University of Allahabad, which is the third oldest university in India. The Institute admits nearly 900 students to undergraduate programs and another 200+ students to postgraduate programs every year. MNNIT has specific > Rules and Guidelines for Branch change at the end of 2 semister.

 

4.National Institute of Technology, DURGAPUR

18.National Institute of Technology, Raipur
Established: 1956(2002)
State: Chhattisgarh
NIRF rank on India level: 63
National Institute of Technology Raipur is situated in the capital city of Chhattisgarh State of India. This institute has a glorious past history of around 6 decades. The institute runs 12 undergraduate programs with student intake of 955 and 10 Postgraduate courses including M.C.A. and M.Tech. in Applied Geology with student intake of 255 per year. In terms of yearly intake, this institute is one of the biggest NIT.
